9th- through 12th-grade students from Washington, DC-area schools may apply.

Application Requirements

You will need to submit these three items along with your online application:

  1. A recent photograph of yourself
    • Accepted formats: .JPG, .JPEG, .PNG, .GIF, or .EPS
    • Label your photo as: Last Name_First Name_photo
      (Example: Smith_Jane_photo)
  2. A letter (or video) of recommendation from a teacher, school administrator, BTLH ambassador, or member of the community
    • Accepted formats: .DOC or .PDF less than 4 MB in size
    • Label your recommendation as: Last Name_First Name_rec
      (Example: Smith_Jane_rec)
  3. A video essay (approximately two to five minutes long) or a written essay (500 words or more) that answers these questions:
    • Which photograph(s), artifact(s), or video(s) from the Museum’s main exhibition, The Holocaust, or any of the Museum’s online exhibitions, is/are most memorable or important to you and why?
    • Why do you think it is important to learn about the Holocaust?
    • What do you hope to accomplish as a BTLH ambassador?
    • Why is it important for youth to engage with their communities?
      • Written essay accepted formats: .DOC or .PDF less than 4 MB in size
      • Label your written essay as: Last Name_First Name_essay
        (Example: Smith_Jane_essay)
      • Video essay accepted formats: YouTube or DropBox link
      • Label you video essay as: Last Name First Name BTLH video essay
        (Example: Smith Jane BTLH video essay)
