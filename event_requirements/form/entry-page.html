Questions to Identify Event Requirements

MarCom’s events and registration standards promote and support events at the Museum, around the country, and around the world.

We make sure that anyone who wants to register, attend, and engage with Museum events and related content have exactly what is needed for the optimal sign up and an onsite attendee experience.

We also ensure that all registration and event data is accessible and captured for promotional, onsite, and historical reference.

Requirements, features, and processes are detailed below for the internal USHMM reference.

The below questions highlight the first batch of key details MarCom needs to build, promote, and manage registration throughout the event lifecycle.

Consider the following:

• What kind of event would you like to promote?
• Is this virtual? Is this in person? Is it both?
• Is the event private or public?
• Is the event free or is there a fee required?
• Do you want to post the event to the USHMM events calendar?
• Who is attending your event?
• How do you want attendees to learn and view information about your event?
• What kind of registration experience would you like the user to have?
• How many registrants and/or applicants will sign up? Estimated number of attendees?
• Do you want to collect specific information about your attendees?
• What are your expectations when it comes to communicating with registrants and attendees?
• Have you submitted the event to the Shared Services Event Coding Request Log?
• Have you contacted the Special Events Team about your event?
• How are you planning to manage onsite registration?
• Will the program be livestreamed? Will the program be recorded? Do you plan to share the recording after the event?
• Do you have any events or event pages, registration form questions for reference that you like to share?
• Will this event be promoted on any social media channels? Regional Office or Program Office social media accounts?
• Any special event promotions we should prepare for or deadlines to consider when it comes to promoting the event?
