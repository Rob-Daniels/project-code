Type Family

The United States Holocaust Memorial Museum primary typefaces are Knockout and Merlo. These typefaces are used to format text in all designed materials. Only the weights displayed in this manual may be used.

The fonts are copyright protected and need to be purchased. Knockout is available for purchase at www.typography.com, and Merlo is available for purchase at www.felicianotypefoundry.com.

Type Treatment

Typography is at the forefront of the Museum identity. The limited identity toolkit forces the richness of typography through the use of color, italics, scale, and type style. Please see examples throughout this manual as well as the guidelines below.

Headlines
  • Knockout may be used for headlines.
  • Use colors contrasting to body copy to call out attention
Body Copy
  • In most cases, Merlo is used for body copy.
  • Merlo does not have a bold style. To call out information within body copy, numerous options are available:
    • Use Merlo in a contrasting color (orange against black)
    • Use Knockout to give a sense of boldness
    • Use Knockout in a color
    • Use Merlo in italics
Tracking
  • The preferred tracking of headlines is 0.
  • The preferred tracking of body copy is 0.
  • The tracking of particular lines can be tightened slightly to avoid hyphens or widows. Keep the tracking visibly consistent.
Leading
  • The preferred leading is usually 1 to 2 points larger than the body copy size. The leading is kept tight to achieve an organized, modern feeling.
Hyphens
  • Avoid the use of hyphens. Try to avoid breaking words across lines if possible.
Punctuation
  • Hang punctuation if possible.
Text alignment
  • Copy should all be left aligned.
Spacing
  • Space between paragraphs and sections will vary from piece to piece.
  • Sections should be spaced out in such a way that it makes the information very accessible to the reader.
  • The size of the spaces will also vary depending on the amount of space available.
  • When space between paragraphs is needed, first use one line space.
  • If one line space is too large, use a space after the paragraph. A “space after” such as .05, .075, and .125 points are easy for multiple users to remember.

Text Hierarchy

Follow these general guidelines to create pieces with consistent text hierarchy that users can read easily. Following a hierarchy creates a recognizable order of text across many different pieces.

Headlines
  • Headlines are the largest type element on the page.
  • Headlines are set in “Initial Caps” or “ALL CAPS” and in HTF50 Knockout Welterweight.
  • Headlines are never set in italics.
Subheads
  • Subheads are roughly half or one third the size of headlines.
  • Subheads may be set in:
    • HTF50 Knockout Welterweight
    • Merlo Italic
    • Merlo in a contrasting color to body copy
Body Copy
  • Body copy can be the same size or slightly smaller than subheads.
Captions
  • Captions are the smallest and lightest type element on the page.
  • Part of the caption can be highlighted by using contrasting color.
  • Captions are set in HTF50 Knockout Welterweight.
Footnotes
  • Footnotes are usually the same size as the captions or smaller.
  • Part of the footnote can be highlighted by using contrasting color.
