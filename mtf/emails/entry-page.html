Please do not apply if you’re unable or unwilling to travel to Washington, DC, and attend the full sessions in both 2024 and 2025. 

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

  • Applicants must be full-time secondary (middle school and high school) classroom teachers of history, social studies, foreign languages, English, journalism, or other related disciplines.
  • Applicants must live and teach in the United States.
  • Applicants must have attended the 2023 Belfer National Conference for Educators. 
  • Teachers selected must prioritize the commitment to the fellowship program over other professional development opportunities during the 2024–25 fellowship year (June 2024–July 2025).
  • Applicants must be willing to facilitate a professional development presentation that shares Museum educational resources in their communities during the fellowship year.

PROGRAM BENEFITS

  • Receive a $1,500 stipend to offset travel expenses for each trip to Washington, DC (hotel, daily transportation, and most meals provided) and 
  • A $2,000 honorarium for their work during the Fellowship year, payable after completing all requirements. 
  • You are a Museum Teacher Fellow after you complete the full Fellowship year and meet all program requirements.

APPLICATION TIPS

  • Preview the Museum Teacher Fellowship Program application essay questions here. 
  • Compose your answers in a Google or Word document, then copy and paste them into the application. This will ensure that you can revise and edit your responses before submission, retain a copy for your records, and avoid the system timing out before you apply.
  • Please upload one letter of recommendation before submitting your final application. Additional information about the required letter of recommendation can be found in Section VIII of the application.
